# Martin Holmer

Lead developer of Tax-Calculator (2015-2019). President of Policy Simulation Group.

## Background

- PhD in Economics, MIT
- Decades of experience designing, developing, testing, and using microsimulation models

## Current Role

President and Senior Economist, Policy Simulation Group

## Tax-Calculator

Lead developer 2015-2019 of Tax-Calculator, the open-source US federal income and payroll tax microsimulation model.

Key contributions:
- Core architecture and Python implementation
- CLI tools and API design
- Integration with PSL ecosystem
- Documentation

## Users of Tax-Calculator

"Administrations from both parties, lawmakers, newspapers, and academic researchers"

## International Work

2018: World Bank asked Holmer to work with India's Ministry of Finance Tax Policy Research Unit to adapt Tax-Calculator for Indian individual income tax. Led to development of open-source tax microsimulation framework for rapid development of new country models.

## Significance

Technical architect who made open-source tax microsimulation practical. His work on Tax-Calculator proved that complex tax code could be modeled in accessible, open-source Python.
