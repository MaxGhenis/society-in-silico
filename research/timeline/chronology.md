# Microsimulation Timeline

## 1950s - Origins

- **1957**: Guy Orcutt publishes "A New Type of Socio-economic System"

## 1960s - Early Development

- First implementations of Orcutt's ideas
- Computing limitations constrain ambition

## 1970s - Institutional Growth

- **1970s**: DYNASIM developed at Urban Institute
- Government agencies begin adopting microsimulation
- Data infrastructure improves (CPS, etc.)

## 1980s - Spread

- Interest spreads to Germany, UK, Australia
- [[ann-harding]] and Australian microsimulation
- Smaller-scale models become feasible

## 1990s - Institutionalization

- **1993**: Canberra conference on microsimulation
- IFS solidifies role in UK budget analysis
- TAXSIM established at NBER

## 2000s - Democratization Begins

- Computing power makes models more accessible
- Academic models proliferate
- Web-based calculators emerge

## 2010s - Open Source Turn

- **2011**: OpenFisca launched in France
- Piketty/Saez/Landais French tax simulator
- Growing demand for transparency

## 2020s - AI Era

- **2021**: PolicyEngine founded
- Large language models enter policy space
- Cosilico and automated encoding
- Real-time policy analysis becomes possible
