# CORSIM

Cornell Microsimulation Model - dynamic demographic model for life-course analysis.

## Institution

Cornell University

## Creator

**Steven Caldwell** (1941-2025)
- Philosophy BA from Dartmouth (1964)
- Fulbright to India â†’ social justice focus
- Sociology PhD from Cornell
- Started at Urban Institute (worked on [[dynasim]])
- Founded CORSIM at Cornell in 1987
- Founded Strategic Forecasting consulting
- Passed away June 9, 2025

## History

- **1987**: Initiated, building on DYNASIM experience
- **Base year**: 1960 US Census PUMS (180,000 records)

## Scale

Among the largest microsimulation models:
- ~1,100 equations
- ~7,000 parameters
- ~26 behavioral modules
- Dozens of algorithms

## What It Simulates

- Schooling
- Labor supply
- Demographics
- Risk factors (smoking, alcohol, diabetes)
- Wealth accumulation
- Life events

## Key Difference from DYNASIM

CORSIM is fully dynamic, single integrated model (vs. DYNASIM's modular approach)

## International Influence

CORSIM methodology adapted for:
- **DYNACAN** (Canada)
- **SVERIGE** (Sweden)
- **POLISIM** (US Social Security Administration)
