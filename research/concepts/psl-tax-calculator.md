# Policy Simulation Library / Tax-Calculator

Open-source tax microsimulation ecosystem based at AEI.

## Institution

Open Source Policy Center (OSPC) at American Enterprise Institute (AEI)

## Founder

**Matt Jensen** - Managing director and founder of OSPC. Also lecturer at University of Chicago's Becker Friedman Institute (Open Source Macroeconomics Laboratory).

## Key People

- **Martin Holmer** - President and senior economist at Policy Simulation Group, key Tax-Calculator developer

## Models

- **Tax-Calculator**: Microsimulation of federal individual income and payroll tax
- **Tax-Data**: Prepares microdata for Tax-Calculator
- **Behavior-Response**: Behavioral response modeling
- **Tax-Brain**: Web interface (now in maintenance)
- **Tax-Cruncher**: Individual tax calculator
- **OG-USA**: Open-source large-scale dynamic overlapping generations model

## Validation

Tax-Calculator results compared to:
- Hand calculations using IRS forms
- Dan Feenberg/Ina Shapiro SAS program at NBER (connects to [[taxsim]])

## Users

Scholars inside/outside AEI, other think tanks, governmental agencies

## Significance

First open-source microsimulation model of US individual income tax. Demonstrates open-source policy modeling is viable. PolicyEngine US builds on similar principles.
