# UKMOD

Free, open-source tax-benefit microsimulation model for the UK, maintained by the University of Essex.

## Institution

Centre for Microsimulation and Policy Analysis (CeMPA), University of Essex

## Leadership

- **Project lead**: Professor Mike Brewer
- **Key developers**: Mike Brewer, Kakia Chatsiou, Paola De Agostini, Chrysa Leventi, Sara Reis, Holly Sutherland, Iva Tasseva

## History

- **Genesis**: 2018
- **Funding**: Nuffield Foundation (grant WEL/43597 2018-2021)
- **Lineage**: Spin-off from UK component of [[euromod]]
- **Development history**: 20+ years (counting EUROMOD UK component)

## Key Features

- **Free and open-source** (unlike IPPR model)
- Separate policy systems for England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland
- Updated twice yearly (Spring/Autumn)
- Uses same platform and simulation engine as EUROMOD

## Notable Users

- Scottish Parliament Information Centre (SPICe)
- NHS Health Scotland
- Welsh Government

## Significance

Demonstrates that high-quality, open tax-benefit modeling is viable. Direct competitor/alternative to proprietary models like IPPR/PERU. Relevant context for PolicyEngine UK.
