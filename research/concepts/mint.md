# MINT

Modeling Income in the Near Term - Social Security Administration's microsimulation model.

## Institution

Social Security Administration (SSA), Office of Research, Evaluation, and Statistics

## Development Partners

- Brookings Institution
- RAND Corporation
- Urban Institute

## Purpose

Project future retirement incomes:
- Marital trends
- Social Security benefits
- Income and poverty
- Effects of policy changes

## Current Version

MINT8 (uses 2023 Trustees Report assumptions)

## Data Sources

- SSA administrative records
- Survey of Income and Program Participation (SIPP)
- Matched together

## Key Uses

- Distributional forecasts of Social Security changes
- Used in National Commission on Fiscal Responsibility and Reform report
- Policy option analysis

## Limitations

- Annual earnings only (can't capture monthly rules like earnings test)
- Single claiming age per person
- No sophisticated claiming strategies
- No disability diagnostic details

## Relationship to DYNASIM

Both project retirement outcomes; MINT is SSA's internal model while [[dynasim]] is at Urban Institute. They often produce complementary analyses.
