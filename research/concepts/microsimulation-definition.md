# Microsimulation

## Definition

Microsimulation is a modeling technique that operates on individual units (people, households, firms) rather than aggregates. It simulates how policy rules affect each unit in a sample, then aggregates results.

## Key Characteristics

1. **Unit of analysis**: Individuals or households, not economy-wide aggregates
2. **Rule-based**: Applies policy rules (tax code, benefit eligibility) to each unit
3. **Data-driven**: Uses survey or administrative data for realistic population
4. **Distributional**: Can answer "who gains, who loses" questions

## Contrast with Macroeconomic Models

| Aspect | Microsimulation | Macro Models |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Unit | Individual | Economy |
| Output | Distribution | Averages |
| Data | Survey/admin | National accounts |
| Questions | Distributional impact | GDP effects |

## Types

- **Static**: No behavioral response, arithmetic only
- **Behavioral**: Models how people change behavior in response to policy
- **Dynamic**: Projects forward in time with demographic changes

## Examples

- TAXSIM (NBER, US)
- IFS models (UK)
- OpenFisca (international)
- PolicyEngine (US, UK)
