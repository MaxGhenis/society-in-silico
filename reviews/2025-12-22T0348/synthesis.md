## Editorial Decision: REVISE AND RESUBMIT

The manuscript shows strong potential but requires significant revision before publication.

## Top 3 Revision Priorities

### 1. Separate Proven from Speculative Work (All 5 reviewers)

**Problem**: Chapters 8 (Cosilico) and 11-13 (Opinion/Democracy/Values) blur the line between validated tools and experimental prototypes.

**Solutions**:
- Create explicit Part IV: "Research Directions" for speculative chapters
- Add visual markers distinguishing validated vs. preliminary vs. theoretical work
- Either remove Cosilico chapter or reframe as "Future Work" appendix

### 2. Add Independent Validation and Uncertainty Quantification (Policy Wonk, Academic, Tech)

**Problem**: PolicyEngine claims lack third-party validation. Point estimates lack confidence intervals despite Chapter 10's excellent uncertainty discussion.

**Solutions**:
- Add head-to-head comparisons: PolicyEngine vs. CBO/JCT/Tax Policy Center
- Include error bars on all budget/impact estimates
- Publish peer-reviewed validation studies and cite them

### 3. Engage with Relevant Academic Literatures (Academic, EA/AI Safety)

**Problem**: Missing engagement with:
- Political science on voter information (Lupia, McCubbins, Achen & Bartels)
- Public finance on benefit cliffs (Hoynes, Rothstein)
- AI alignment (CEV, Goodhart's Law, moral uncertainty)
- Moral philosophy (Singer, Greene, Parfit)

**Solutions**:
- Add literature review sections to Chapters 11-13
- Connect value forecasting to Coherent Extrapolated Volition
- Engage with social choice impossibility results

4. **Long-term**: Return to Cosilico chapter only after product launch and validation

## Conclusion

The core vision is compelling and the historical chapters are publication-ready. Part I and Chapter 10 are excellent. Part II (PolicyEngine) needs validation. Part III (speculative) needs major restructuring or should be positioned as a research agenda rather than conclusions.

With revision, this could be an important book that changes how people think about policy analysis and democratic participation. Without revision, it risks being dismissed as self-promotional speculation.
