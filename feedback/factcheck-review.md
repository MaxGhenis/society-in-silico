### GPT-4 Tax Accuracy (Line 47)

**Claim**: "GPT-4 achieves only 67% accuracy on basic tax questions"

**Status**: ✓ **VERIFIED**

**Sources**:
- Primary source: Blair-Stanek et al. (2023), "OpenAI Cribbed Our Tax Example, But Can GPT-4 Really Do Tax?" Available at [arXiv:2309.09992](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.09992)
- [Tax Notes - Featured Analysis](https://www.taxnotes.com/featured-analysis/openai-cribbed-our-tax-example-can-gpt-4-really-do-tax/2023/08/11/7h0hc) - confirms "Researchers posed all 276 true/false tax cases to GPT-4... GPT-4 got 186 correct, amounting to 67 percent accuracy"
- [Going Concern - GPT-4 Answers Tax Questions](https://www.goingconcern.com/gpt-4-answers-tax-questions-gets-them-mostly-right/)

**Additional Context**: The study used 276 true/false tax cases with the full Internal Revenue Code provided. The researchers found that "not one of the errors was mathematical; all involved misreading the statutes."

**Claim**: "DYNASIM's mainframe ambitions"

**Related context from research notes**: Guy Orcutt research note states "1975: First DYNASIM version completed" and "1969: Begins DYNASIM work at Urban Institute"

**Status**: ⚠ **MINOR DISCREPANCY FOUND**

**Sources**:
- [Society of Actuaries - DYNASIM Chapter](https://www.soa.org/493824/globalassets/assets/files/research/projects/chapter_3.pdf) - confirms "In 1969, work began on DYNASIM at the Urban Institute under the direction of Guy Orcutt, and the first version was completed in 1975"

**Recommendation**: Use "1975" as the completion date (most common in sources) but acknowledge development continued through 1976 if specificity is needed.

## Potential Issues / Corrections Needed ✗

### "Nuclear Fire" vs. Other Descriptions

**Line 29**: "watched Paris destroyed by nuclear fire"

**Status**: ✗ **TERMINOLOGY INCONSISTENCY**

**Finding**: Westworld sources variously describe the Paris incident as:
- "nuclear strike" (Westworld Wiki - France)
- "thermonuclear accident" (Den of Geek)
- "thermonuclear incident" (NME, trailer)
- "nuclear bomb" or "nuclear explosion" (Inverse, CBR)

**Issue**: "Nuclear fire" is evocative and accurate but not the terminology used in the show itself.

**Recommendation**:
- Option 1: Change to "nuclear strike" or "thermonuclear incident" for precision
- Option 2: Keep "nuclear fire" as more literary but add footnote explaining the show's terminology
- Option 3: Use "nuclear destruction" as neutral term

**Severity**: Minor - this is stylistic rather than factually incorrect
